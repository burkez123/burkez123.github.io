History of the Dallas Cowboys

The Dallas Cowboys, founded in the year 1960 by Clint Murchinson Jr., was the first football team south of Washington D.C. since 1952 when the Dallas Texans dissolved, and the Washington Redskins owner held a monopoly on the entire South. After tense negotiations, some backstabbing between the Washington Redskins owner and Murchinson, he was allowed to start a football team in Texas, and thus a rivalry between the Cowboys and Redskins was born. The Dallas Cowboys played in their homefield The Cotton Bowl Stadium for their first 11 years as a team. In that time, they made 4 Conference Championships, 5 Division championships, 6 playoff apeearances and won the Superbowl once (in 1971). In 1971, they left the Cotton Bowl Stadium for the smaller yet more robust Texas Stadium and stayed there until 2008, through the 70s, the Cowboys set a new record for winning a total of 105 regular season games. In the time they were at the Texas Stadium, they won 4 Superbowls, made 23 playoff appearances, won 14 Division championships, and 6 Conference championships. As of today, they play at the AT&T Stadium.
